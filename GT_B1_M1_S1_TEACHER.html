🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · If I Were the CEO…

Teacher says: "You are the CEO of your company for one day. You have unlimited power and budget. Tell me 3 things you would change immediately." Student responds freely. Teacher notes errors and models correct Second Conditional without interrupting. Then switch: teacher plays CEO, student asks "What would you do if…?" questions.

Why: the Second Conditional becomes real and personal when it connects to the student's actual workplace.
If I were the CEO, I'd restructure the team.
If we had a bigger budget, we'd invest in training.
What would you do if a key client left?
I'd change the communication process if I could.
If the company were more flexible, people would stay longer.
I wouldn't have so many meetings if it were up to me.

📐Grammar — Second Conditional — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
Key concept: Second Conditional = IMAGINARY. The situation is NOT real or is very unlikely. The most common errors: using 'would' in the if-clause, and using present tense instead of past.
📐 Second Conditional — Structure
If + past simple, would + base verb
✅ Affirmative
If I were the CEO, I would change everything.
If we had more time, we'd do it better.
❌ Negative
If I didn't have to work, I'd travel.
If she weren't so busy, she'd help.
❓ Question
What would you do if you lost your job?
Would you accept it if they offered?
💡 Key: This is IMAGINARY — the situation is NOT real or very unlikely. Compare: "If I win" (possible) vs. "If I won" (imaginary).

Critical rules
If + past simple (NOT would)
would + base verb (NOT would + past)
❌ If I would have more time…
❌ If I am the CEO…
💡 Native speaker note
'If I were you' — native speakers use WERE for all persons (I/he/she/it) in formal Second Conditional. 'If I was' is common in informal speech but 'were' is always correct.

Let me tell you about something that happened at work last year that made me think: 'What if¿qué pasaría si we did this completely differently?' Our team had been struggling with a strategyestrategia that clearly wasn't working. We were losing clients, morale was low, and nobody seemed to have any real proposalspropuestas for how to fix it.

One day in a meeting, I finally said: "If I were in charge of this,"Si yo estuviera a cargo de esto "I would scrap the current approach entirely." The room went quiet. Then my manager said: 'OK — what would you do if you hadtuvieras full authority and a bigger budgetpresupuesto?' I hadn't expected that. But I had been thinking about it for months. I proposed a complete overhaul of our client communication system.

I said: 'If we simplifiedsimplificáramos the process and gave the team more autonomy, we'd see results within 90 days.' They gave me the opportunityoportunidad. And honestly? It worked. Looking back, if I hadn't spoken up in that meeting, nothing would have changed. Sometimes the most powerful thing you can do is say: "What if we tried something different?"¿qué tal si intentamos algo diferente?

🗣 Colloquial English — B1
"Think outside the box"
to think creatively and go beyond conventional solutions
If we really thought outside the box, we'd find a much better approach to this problem.
"That's a no-brainer"
something that is completely obvious and requires no serious thought
If they offered me that salary, accepting it would be a no-brainer.
"Let's throw some ideas at the wall"
to suggest multiple ideas freely without filtering, to see what works
If we had an hour, I'd love to throw some ideas at the wall and see what sticks.
"I'd rather"
to express a preference — what you would prefer to do instead
I'd rather have one focused meeting than three unfocused ones.

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — B1 · Second Conditional
❌ If I would have more resources, I'd change the strategy.
→
✅ If I had more resources, I'd change the strategy.
Never use 'would' in the if-clause. Use past simple: had, were, knew, could.
❌ If I am the manager, I will change everything.
→
✅ If I were the manager, I would change everything.
This is imaginary — use past simple (were) + would, not present + will.
❌ What would you do if you will lose the client?
→
✅ What would you do if you lost the client?
Past simple in the if-clause, not future. 'Lost' not 'will lose'.
