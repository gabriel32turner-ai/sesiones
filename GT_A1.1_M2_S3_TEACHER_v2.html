For this/that/these/those: use real objects. Point at something close: This is a ___. Point far: That is a ___. Physical demonstration is worth more than explanation.
Key Words
WordMeaning (español)Example
this 🔊one thing close to you (este / esta (cerca))This is my laptop.
that 🔊one thing far from you (ese / esa (lejos))That is the printer over there.
these 🔊2+ things close (estos / estas (cerca))These are my files.
those 🔊2+ things far (esos / esas (lejos))Those are the meeting rooms.
big / large 🔊a lot of space (grande)Our building is very big.
small 🔊not a lot of space (pequeño/a)My office is small but comfortable.
new / old 🔊recent / not recent (nuevo/a / viejo/a)My chair is new. The printer is old.
modern 🔊new style, up to date (moderno/a)Our office is modern and open plan.
Key Verbs
WordMeaning (español)Example
point at 🔊to indicate with finger (señalar)She pointed at the map.
show 🔊to let someone see (mostrar)Can you show me your workspace?
describe 🔊to explain what something is like (describir)Describe your office in English.
prefer 🔊to like one thing more (preferir)I prefer a quiet office.
look like 🔊to have a similar appearance (parecerse a)What does your office look like?
have 🔊to own or possess (tener)Do you have a private office?
need 🔊to require something (necesitar)I need a bigger desk.
compare 🔊to show similarities and differences (comparar)Compare your desk with mine.

⚡Wrap-up and Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A1.1 · This/That/Adjectives
❌ These is my desk.
→
✅ This is my desk.
Desk = 1 thing → THIS (singular). These = plural only.
❌ My office is very comfort.
→
✅ My office is very comfortable.
Comfort is a noun. The adjective is comfortable.
❌ That chairs are new.
→
✅ Those chairs are new.
Chairs = plural + far → THOSE (not that).
