🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · Did You or Didn't You?

Teacher says a statement. Student either confirms (Yes, I did.) or denies with a full negative sentence. Example — Teacher: "You went to Guadalajara last week." Student: "No, I didn't go to Guadalajara. I stayed in Celaya."

Why: forces production of negative past — the hardest structure for Spanish speakers.

Key Words
WordMeaning (español)Example

WordMeaning (español)Example

📐Grammar — Past Negatives and Questions — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
The key insight: didn't and Did carry ALL the past meaning — the main verb always stays in base form. Drill this until it's automatic.
📐 Past Negatives and Questions
❌ Negative
Subject + didn't + base verb
I didn't finish the report.
She didn't go to the meeting.
They didn't have time.
❓ Question
Did + subject + base verb?
Did you finish the report?
Did she go to the meeting?
Why didn't they have time?
❌
I didn't went.  ·  She didn't had time.  ·  Did you went to the meeting?
Wh- Questions in Past
What did you do?
What did you work on?
Where did you go?
Where did you go last week?
When did it happen?
When did the meeting finish?
Who did you call?
Who did you speak to?
Why didn't you come?
Why didn't you attend?
How did it go?
How did the presentation go?

Critical rule
AFTER DIDN'T OR DID → always BASE verb
✅ She didn't go
✅ Did she go?
❌ She didn't went
❌ Did she went?

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A1.2 · Negatives and Questions
❌ I didn't went to the meeting.
→
✅ I didn't go to the meeting.
After didn't → base verb always. went is past, but didn't already shows past.
❌ She not finished the report.
→
✅ She didn't finish the report.
In English, negatives use didn't + base verb. Not just 'not' before the verb.
❌ Did you understood the contract?
→
✅ Did you understand the contract?
After Did → base verb always. understood is past, but Did already shows past.
