Key Verbs for Self-Description
WordMeaning (español)Example
tend to 🔊to usually do something (soler / tender a)I tend to work best under pressure.
come across as 🔊to seem or appear to others (parecer / dar la impresion de)She comes across as very confident.
be known for 🔊to be recognized for a quality (ser conocido por)He's known for being extremely reliable.
consider oneself 🔊to think of oneself as (considerarse)I consider myself a collaborative leader.
work well under 🔊to perform well in a condition (funcionar bien bajo)I work well under pressure.
handle 🔊to deal with something (manejar)She handles conflict very calmly.
prefer 🔊to like better (preferir)I tend to prefer working alone on complex tasks.
avoid 🔊to deliberately not do (evitar)He tends to avoid confrontation — sometimes too much.

📐Grammar — Tend To + Verb — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
Tend to = a softer, more sophisticated way to describe habits than always/never. It's natural in English interviews and performance reviews. Push for this.
📐 Tend To + Verb — Describing Habits
Subject + tend(s) to + base verb
I tend to / She tends to / People tend to
= a general habit or usual behavior
About yourself
I tend to arrive early.
I tend to avoid conflict.
I tend to work better alone.
About others
She tends to be direct.
He tends to take on too much.
Our team tends to collaborate well.
❌
I tend work early. · She tends avoid conflict. · He is tend to be direct.

My name is Valeria and I've worked in Human Resources for eight years. People tend to describe me as empatheticempatica and directdirecta. I think both are true. I listen first, but I say what I think — clearly and fairly. In my experience, the most effectiveefectivos HR professionals tend to combine strong interpersonalinterpersonales skills with analytical thinking.

One weakness I've worked on is being too perfectionistperfeccionista — I've learned to let go of details that don't matter. Tell me about yourself and how you work. — Sure. I've been in project management for about six years now. I consider myself quite organized and calm under pressure — which is kind of essential in my field. I tend to break big projects into smaller milestones because I think it's easier to manage and easier to track progress.

How would your team describe you? — I think they'd say I'm reliable and collaborative. I've never been the type to take all the credit — I believe in sharing wins. I do tend to be direct, though. If something isn't working, I'll say so — but I try to frame it constructively. People say I give good feedback because I'm honest but I'm also empathetic.

What's an area you're still working on? — I tend to take on too much. I find it hard to say no. I've been working on delegating more and trusting my team. It's been a real growth area for me in the last year.

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A2.2 · Personality & Tend To
❌ I tend be direct.
→
✅ I tend to be direct.
Tend to always needs TO before the verb. Never: tend + verb directly.
❌ She is very ambition.
→
✅ She is very ambitious.
Ambitious is the adjective. Ambition is the noun. Use the adjective to describe people.
❌ People say I'm a reliable person man.
→
✅ People say I'm reliable.
Adjective alone is enough. No need for 'person' or 'man' after the adjective.
