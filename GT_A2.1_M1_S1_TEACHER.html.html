In A2.1 you say: I'm going to / I'll / We've decided to.
🎯 Goal: talk about plans and decisions in meetings
🎯 Grammar: going to + will (S2)
🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · Your Real Meeting Agenda

Student tells you 3 things they are going to do this week at work. Teacher writes the going to structure in chat for each one. Then reverse: teacher says a plan, student converts it to going to. Focus: it must be a REAL plan, not invented.

Why: going to is for pre-planned decisions. Real plans = instant comprehension of the grammar's meaning.
I'm going to call the client on Monday.
We're going to present the new proposal.
She's going to follow up with the supplier.
They're going to reschedule the kickoff.
I'm not going to attend — I have a conflict.
Are you going to send the agenda?

📐Grammar — Going To: Plans — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
Going to = pre-planned decision, evidence-based prediction. Keep it simple for S1: focus on affirmative and question. Negative in practice. Will contrast comes in S2.
📐 Going To — Confirmed Plans
Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb
I am going to · He/She/It is going to · We/You/They are going to
✅ Affirmative
I'm going to call the client.
We're going to reschedule.
❌ Negative
I'm not going to attend.
She's not going to present.
❓ Question
Are you going to follow up?
Is he going to present?
❌
I going to call.  ·  She going to present.  ·  We are go to reschedule.

Quick rule
Going to = pre-planned
I am / He is / We are + going to
❌ I going to / She going to
💡 Key concept
Going to = the plan already exists BEFORE you speak. You decided earlier. Compare with will (S2) = decision made at the moment of speaking.

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A2.1 · Going To
❌ I going to send the report.
→
✅ I am going to send the report.
Going to always needs the verb to be: am/is/are. Never drop it.
❌ We are go to reschedule.
→
✅ We are going to reschedule.
It's going to — not just go to. The -ing is part of the structure.
❌ She is going to presented the results.
→
✅ She is going to present the results.
After going to → always use the BASE verb. Not the past or -ed form.
