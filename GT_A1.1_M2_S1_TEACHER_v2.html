🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · "Desk Scavenger Hunt"

Ask the student to find and name 5 objects on their desk in English. Teacher provides any unknown word immediately. Then ask: "Is that your pen? Is that a laptop?" — student answers yes/no using to be.

Why: real objects = real vocabulary. The student will remember "keyboard" forever because they touched theirs during this activity.

📐 Possessive Adjectives — my / your / his / her / our / their
I → my  ·  you → your  ·  he → his  ·  she → her  ·  we → our  ·  they → their

Is this you desk?  ·  She laptop is new.  ·  He manager is Carlos.

📖Reading + Comprehension⏱ 8–10 min

My name is Ana. I work at a small company. My office is on the third floor.

My desk is next to the window. I have a laptop, a keyboard, and a phone on my desk. My chair is new and comfortable.

Our office is not big. My manager's desk is near the door. His computer is old but it works well. We share a printer. It is on the second floor.

Comprehension questions
1. What floor is Ana's office on?
2. What does Ana have on her desk? (name 3 things)
3. Is Ana's chair new or old?
4. Where is the manager's desk?
5. Where is the printer?
Key: 1. Third floor · 2. Laptop, keyboard, phone · 3. New · 4. Near the door · 5. Second floor
Photo
Implicit grammar
To be: is, are throughout
Possessives: my, our, his
Simple present: works, share
Adjectives: small, new, old, comfortable

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A1.1 · Office & Possessives
❌ Is this you desk?
→
✅ Is this your desk?
'You' = pronoun. 'Your' = possessive. Never use 'you' as a possessive.
❌ She laptop is new.
→
✅ Her laptop is new.
She = pronoun. Her = possessive. I/my · He/his · She/her — always.
❌ The office have 3 floors.
→
✅ The office has 3 floors.
It/He/She → has (not have). Have = I/You/We/They.
