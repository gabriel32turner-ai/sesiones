Key Nouns — M1
WordDefinitionExample
🔊 hypothesisan idea or explanation that is not yet proven, used as a starting pointThe whole discussion was based on a hypothesis — what if the market collapsed?
🔊 scenarioa possible situation or sequence of eventsLet's consider this scenario: you lose your biggest client. What do you do?
🔊 conditiona situation that must exist before something else can happenThe condition for success would be having the right team in place.
🔊 consequencethe result or effect of an actionIf we rushed this decision, the consequences would be difficult to reverse.
🔊 assumptionsomething accepted as true without proof, used as a basis for reasoningIf your assumption were correct, the whole strategy would need to change.
🔊 riskthe possibility that something bad or undesirable will happenIf you don't assess the risk now, you'd face bigger problems later.
🔊 opportunitya set of circumstances that makes it possible to do somethingIf we recognized this opportunity sooner, we'd already be three steps ahead.
🔊 outcomethe result or consequence of an action or eventIf both teams collaborated, the outcome would be far better than working separately.
Key Verbs — M1
WordDefinitionExample
🔊 imagineto form a picture in your mind of a situation that is not realImagine you were offered your dream job tomorrow. What would you do?
🔊 supposeto assume for the purpose of argument or discussionSuppose the strategy failed — what would your backup plan be?
🔊 wishto want something that is not currently real or possibleI wish the process were simpler — it creates unnecessary delays.
🔊 preferto like one thing more than anotherI'd prefer to work on one complex project than manage ten smaller ones.
🔊 adviseto give a recommendation about what someone should doIf I were advising you, I'd say: address it before it becomes a conflict.
🔊 proposeto formally suggest a plan or course of actionWhat would you propose if the current approach stopped working?
🔊 adaptto change in response to new conditionsIf the market shifted, how quickly would your team adapt?
🔊 considerto think carefully about something before making a decisionIf you considered all the variables, which option would you choose?

📖Reading — M1 Final Text⏱ 10–12 min
💡 Click a sentence to highlight · Click underlined word for Spanish

I want to end this module with a question that I think is worth sitting with: "If you already knewsi ya supieras what you know now about your career, your industry, and yourself — what would you do differently?" Most people, when they hear that question, have an instant answer. Not because they regret their path — but because they know, deep down, that there were moments when they wished they had been bolder.

They wish they had proposed that idea. They wish they had asked for that promotion sooner. They wish they had trusted their instincts more. And here's what I've come to believe: the gap between where we are and where we wish we were is almost never a gap of ability or talent. It's a gap of imaginationimaginacion. Of permission. Of willingness to ask: "What if I tried?"¿y si lo intento? The Second Conditional isn't just a grammar structure. It's a mindset.

The most successful people I know are not the ones with the best credentialscredenciales. They're the ones who regularly ask themselves: 'If I could do this differently, what would I do?' And then — and this is the part that matters — they actually do something about it. If you walked away from this module with one thing, I'd want it to be this: keep asking 'what if?' Because the answer to that question might just change everything.

I want to ask you something personal. If you could go back to the moment you took your current job — knowing everything you know now — would you still take it? — That's a powerful question. Honestly? Yes. But I'd approach it completely differently. I'd negotiate harder from day one. I wish I had asked for a clearer role definition. And I'd build the key relationships much earlier. — What would those early relationships have changed? — Everything, probably. If I had invested in those connections sooner, I'd have had a much stronger platform to propose ideas. Instead, I spent two years wishing things were different when I should have been building the influence to actually change them.

If you could give your younger self one piece of advice — just one — what would it be? — I'd say: stop waiting for someone to give you permission. If I were twenty-eight again, I'd speak up in every meeting. I'd share every idea, even the half-formed ones. Because the people who get noticed are not necessarily the smartest — they're the most present. The ones who make others think. — That resonates. What would you do if you were in my position right now — early career, ambitious, but feeling like you're being held back? — If I were in your shoes, I'd do three things. First, I'd identify the one person in the organization who could really accelerate my growth and invest deeply in that relationship. Second, I'd stop trying to prove I'm capable and start proposing — ideas, solutions, improvements. And third — I wouldn't wait for the perfect moment. There is no perfect moment. The moment you act is the moment you create.

Last question: if you could change one thing about the way companies develop talent, what would it be? — I wish companies invested more in honest conversations. Not performance reviews — real conversations. If managers had genuine, regular exchanges with their teams about what they wish were different, what they'd change if they could, what they'd do if they weren't afraid — the talent would stay. The ideas would flow. And the companies that did that would be so far ahead of the curve that the others would struggle to compete. That, to me, would be a game changer. A real no-brainer, honestly.

"Think outside the box"
to approach problems creatively, beyond conventional solutions
If we thought outside the box, we'd find an approach nobody in the industry has tried.
"That's a no-brainer"
something so obviously the right choice that it requires no serious thought
If the opportunity is there and the risk is low, taking it is a no-brainer.
"If I were in your shoes"
empathetic advice — imagining yourself in someone else's situation
If I were in your shoes, I'd make the decision sooner rather than later.
"Ahead of the curve"
more advanced than others, preparing for what hasn't happened yet
If we invested in this now, we'd be well ahead of the curve when the market shifts.
