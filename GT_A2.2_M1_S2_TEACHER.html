🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · Past or Perfect?

Teacher reads a sentence WITHOUT the verb tense. Student decides: present perfect or simple past? Key question: "Is there a specific time mentioned? If yes → simple past. If the experience is still relevant now → present perfect."

Why: this is the most common A2.2 error — Spanish speakers use simple past for everything.
PP or Past?
I ___ (work) in logistics for 5 years. (still there)
Clue: still true now → PP
PP or Past?
She ___ (join) the company in 2019.
Clue: specific year → Past
PP or Past?
We ___ (launch) our new product last quarter.
Clue: last quarter = specific time → Past
PP or Past?
Have you ever ___ (manage) a team?
Clue: experience, no time = PP
PP or Past?
He ___ (move) to Mexico City three years ago.
Clue: three years ago = specific → Past

📐Grammar — PP vs Simple Past — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
The clearest test: does the sentence have a specific finished time? (in 2020, last year, ago) → Simple past. Is it still true or relevant now? → Present perfect.
📐 Present Perfect vs Simple Past
PRESENT PERFECT — Link to NOW
Experience / result still relevant NOW
I've worked here for 3 years. (still here)
She's led many projects. (general experience)
No specific time / up to now
SIMPLE PAST — Finished Action
Specific time / completed event
I joined the company in 2020.
She led the merger project last year.

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A2.2 · PP vs Simple Past
❌ I worked here for 3 years. (still here)
→
✅ I've worked here for 3 years.
For + ongoing situation = present perfect. Simple past = finished.
❌ I have joined in 2020.
→
✅ I joined in 2020.
Specific time (in 2020) = simple past. Present perfect never uses in/last/ago.
❌ Did you ever work abroad?
→
✅ Have you ever worked abroad?
Ever = life experience = present perfect. Use Have/Has, not Did.
