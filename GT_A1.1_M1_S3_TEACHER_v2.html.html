🟠Vocabulary — click a word to hear it⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
Always connect to real context: 'Where are your clients from? What language do they speak?' Every word should connect to their actual job.
Key Words
WordMeaning (español)Example
country 🔊a nation with its own land (país)My country is Mexico.
nationality 🔊the country you are from (nacionalidad)My nationality is Mexican.
language 🔊what people speak (idioma)The language in Brazil is Portuguese.
capital 🔊the main city of a country (capital)The capital of Mexico is Mexico City.
city 🔊a large town (ciudad)I live in the city of Celaya.
continent 🔊a very large area of land (continente)Mexico is in North America.
international 🔊between different countries (internacional)My company has international clients.
client 🔊a customer or business partner (cliente)My clients are from the USA.
Key Verbs
WordMeaning (español)Example
come from 🔊to be originally from (ser de / venir de)I come from Guanajuato.
live in 🔊to have your home (vivir en)I live in Celaya.
work with 🔊to do business with (trabajar con)We work with clients from the USA.
speak 🔊to use a language (hablar)My clients speak English.
travel to 🔊to go to a place (viajar a)I travel to Mexico City for meetings.
be from 🔊to have origin in a place (ser de)She is from Germany.
export 🔊to sell products to other countries (exportar)We export products to the USA.
import 🔊to buy products from other countries (importar)We import parts from Japan.

📐Grammar — To Be + Nationality — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
Focus on: verb to be + nationality. Most common error: 'She Germany' (drops verb AND uses country instead of nationality). Two problems in one — fix both.
📐 To Be + Nationality
I am Mexican.  ·  She is German.  ·  They are American.
✅
I am from Mexico. · She is Mexican. · My clients are from the USA.
❌
I Mexican. · She Germany. · My clients from USA.

Quick reference
I → am + Mexican
He/She/It → is + German
We/You/They → are + American
💡 Two errors in one
'She Germany' = missing verb + wrong word. Fix: She IS German (verb + nationality, not country name).

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A1.1 · Countries & Nationalities
❌ She Germany.
→
✅ She is German.
Never drop the verb. AND use the nationality (German), not the country (Germany).
❌ My client is from United States.
→
✅ My client is from the United States.
The USA always needs 'the': the United States, the UK.
❌ I am speak English.
→
✅ I speak English.
Don't use 'am' + verb. Simple present: I speak, not I am speak.
