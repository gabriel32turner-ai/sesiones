🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · Meeting Situations

Teacher presents a meeting situation. Student responds spontaneously with will. Example: "The client just called with a complaint." Student: "I'll call them back right now." Keep it fast — this trains the automatic response that will requires.

Why: will = spontaneous, so it must be practiced AS a spontaneous response — not planned in advance.

Key Words

offer 🔊to say you will do something (ofrecimiento)I'll send you the file right now.

concern 🔊a worry or problem (preocupacion / inquietud)Don't worry — I'll resolve it.

📐Grammar — Will vs Going To — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
The contrast is the core of A2.1 grammar. One clear rule: planned before = going to. Decided now = will. Give students real meeting scenarios to make the choice.
📐 Will vs Going To — The Key Contrast
GOING TO — Pre-planned ✅
Decision made BEFORE speaking.
"I'm going to present the results."
(You planned this yesterday)
WILL — Spontaneous ⚡
Decision made AT THE MOMENT of speaking.
"I'll take care of that."
(Decided just now, in the meeting)
❌
I will present tomorrow. (planned → going to)  ·  I'm going to take that! (spontaneous → will)

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A2.1 · Will vs Going To
❌ I will present on Friday. (it was planned)
→
✅ I am going to present on Friday.
If it was planned before → going to. Will is only for decisions made right now.
❌ I'm going to take care of that! (spontaneous)
→
✅ I'll take care of that!
Spontaneous offer in the meeting → will, not going to.
❌ Will you going to attend?
→
✅ Are you going to attend? / Will you attend?
Never mix will and going to. Pick one: Are you going to...? OR Will you...?
