🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · Meeting Control Roleplay

Teacher starts speaking about a work topic and goes OFF the agenda. Student interrupts politely and redirects. Then teacher says something unclear — student asks for clarification. Finally teacher lets the meeting run over — student wraps it up.

Why: these are the three most common meeting management challenges. Practice them as real scenarios, not drills.

📐Grammar — Adverbs of Manner: clearly, efficiently, directly — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
Short grammar focus. Adverbs of manner describe HOW something is done. At A2.1 the most useful are: clearly, efficiently, directly, professionally, effectively. They immediately elevate meeting language.
📐 Adverbs of Manner
adjective + ly = adverb  ·  clear → clearly · efficient → efficiently · direct → directly · professional → professionally
Position: after the verb or at the end of the sentence
✅
She explained the plan clearly. · He works very efficiently. · Please communicate directly.
❌
She explained the plan clear. · He works very efficient. · Please be directly.

📖Reading + Comprehension⏱ 8–10 min
💡 Click a sentence to highlight · Click underlined word for translation

Good meetings don't just happen — they are managedadministradas / conducidas. A good chairpersonmoderador opens clearlyclaramente, states the agenda, and keeps the discussion on tracken curso. When someone goes off topic, they say: 'That's interesting. Can we add that to the next agenda?'

When the discussion gets unclearpoco claro, a good meeting leader asks: 'Could you clarify what you mean by that?' If someone wants to interruptinterrumpir, the polite way is: 'Sorry to interrupt — can I just add one thing?' When a meeting is running overpasandose del tiempo, the leader says: 'We're running over. Let's wrap up and assign the action points quickly.'

At the end, a great meeting always finishes with a recaprecapitulacion and clear next steps. The most effective meetings are short, focused, and well-prepared.

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A2.1 · Meeting Language
❌ I want make a point.
→
✅ I'd like to make a point. / Can I make a point?
After want/would like → use the infinitive. Also: I'd like is more professional than I want in meetings.
❌ She explained clear.
→
✅ She explained clearly.
Clearly is an adverb (how she explained). You need -ly. Clear is an adjective.
❌ Can you clarify me this point?
→
✅ Could you clarify this point?
Clarify is not used with indirect objects. Just: clarify + the thing, not clarify me.
