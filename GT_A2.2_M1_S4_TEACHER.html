Work Benefits
WordMeaning (español)Example
flexible hours 🔊ability to choose your work schedule (horario flexible)The company offers flexible hours — I start at 8 or 10.
remote work 🔊working from outside the office (trabajo remoto / home office)We've already implemented a remote work policy.
health insurance 🔊medical coverage paid by employer (seguro medico)The benefits include full health insurance.
bonus 🔊extra payment above salary (bono)We get a performance bonus twice a year.
vacation days 🔊paid time off from work (dias de vacaciones)We get 20 vacation days per year.
wellness program 🔊company health initiative (programa de bienestar)Our wellness program includes a gym allowance.
professional development 🔊training and growth opportunities (desarrollo profesional)The company invests in our professional development.
team building 🔊activities to strengthen team bonds (team building / integracion)We do team building activities every quarter.
Hobbies & Free Time
WordMeaning (español)Example
be into 🔊to enjoy or be enthusiastic about (estar muy interesado en / ser fan de)I'm really into photography in my free time.
be passionate about 🔊to love and care deeply about (ser apasionado por)She's passionate about social entrepreneurship.
enjoy 🔊to get pleasure from (disfrutar)I enjoy hiking on weekends — it clears my head.
recently started 🔊began something not long ago (recientemente empece)I've recently started learning to cook.
in my free time 🔊when I'm not working (en mi tiempo libre)In my free time I read business books and run.
spend time 🔊to use time on an activity (pasar tiempo (en/haciendo))I spend most of my free time with my family.
give back 🔊to help the community (retribuir / devolver algo)I volunteer on weekends — I like to give back.
relax 🔊to rest and reduce stress (relajarse)After long weeks, I relax with music and cooking.

Linking work + life
Professional tip: In English-speaking work cultures, sharing hobbies builds connection. I'm into X sounds natural. My hobby is X sounds like a school essay. Use: I'm into / passionate about / recently started.

📐Grammar — Present Perfect: For / Since / Ever / Never — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
Four key PP adverbs: FOR (duration), SINCE (starting point), EVER (in life), NEVER (not in life). These are essential for self-description and professional conversation.
📐 Present Perfect — For / Since / Ever / Never
FOR
duration of time
I've worked here for 3 years.
SINCE
starting point
I've had this role since 2021.
EVER
in your life (questions)
Have you ever worked abroad?
NEVER
not in your life
I've never asked for a raise.

My company has invested in professional development too — I've completed three certification courses this year. I've never felt so engaged at work. The culture, the benefits, and the people make a real difference.

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A2.2 · For / Since / Ever / Never
❌ I've worked here since 3 years.
→
✅ I've worked here for 3 years.
Since needs a point in time (2020, January). For needs a duration (3 years, 6 months).
❌ I work here for 5 years.
→
✅ I've worked here for 5 years.
Duration with 'for' and still ongoing = present perfect. Not simple present.
❌ Did you ever try yoga?
→
✅ Have you ever tried yoga?
Ever with life experience = present perfect. Use Have, not Did.
