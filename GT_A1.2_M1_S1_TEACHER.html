🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · My Week in 5 Verbs

Teacher writes 5 work verbs in chat: WORK / CALL / ATTEND / SEND / FINISH. Student uses each one to say something they did this week: "I worked on a proposal." If they say the verb wrong, teacher writes the corrected form — no explanation, just the model.

Why: activates past tense production immediately. Student speaks before they study.
WORK
Past: worked → I worked on a report.
CALL
Past: called → I called a client.
ATTEND
Past: attended → I attended a meeting.
SEND
Past: sent → I sent 20 emails.
FINISH
Past: finished → I finished the project.

📐Grammar — Simple Past: Regular Verbs — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
Rule is simple: verb + ed. Most errors come from forgetting -ed entirely ('I work yesterday') or pronunciation. Spend 2 min on rule, 8 min on practice.

Quick rule
Affirmative: verb + ed
Negative: didn't + verb
Question: Did + subject + verb?
💡 Most common error
'I work yesterday.' → The verb needs -ed: I WORKED. In negative/questions use the base verb: didn't work, Did you work?

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A1.2 · Simple Past Regular Verbs
❌ I work yesterday on the report.
→
✅ I worked yesterday on the report.
Simple past = verb + ed. 'Yesterday' is a past signal — the verb must change.
❌ She didn't worked late.
→
✅ She didn't work late.
After didn't, use the BASE verb — NOT the past form. Didn't already shows past.
❌ Did you attended the meeting?
→
✅ Did you attend the meeting?
Same rule: Did already shows past. The verb stays in base form.
