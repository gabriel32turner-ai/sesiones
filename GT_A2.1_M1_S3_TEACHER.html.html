🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · Agree or Disagree?

Teacher makes 5 statements about work. Student must agree or disagree AND give a reason. Teacher models first: "In my opinion, short meetings are more productive. I agree because people stay focused." Student must use agree/disagree language, not just yes/no.

Why: activates opinion language immediately in a real context — before teaching the phrases.
"Meetings in English should always have an agenda."
"Remote meetings are less productive than in-person ones."
"The best time for a meeting is Monday morning."
"Short meetings of 30 minutes are always enough."
"Everyone should speak in every meeting."

WordMeaning (español)Example
opinion 🔊what you personally think (opinion)In my opinion, the deadline is too tight.

concern 🔊a worry or doubt (preocupacion / inquietud)My main concern is the budget.
suggestion 🔊an idea to consider (sugerencia)I have a suggestion — what if we split the task?
agreement 🔊when two sides say yes (acuerdo)We reached an agreement on the timeline.

Key Verbs
WordMeaning (español)Example
agree 🔊to have the same opinion (estar de acuerdo)I agree with your assessment.
disagree 🔊to have a different opinion (no estar de acuerdo)I disagree — I think it's too risky.
think 🔊to have an opinion (pensar / creer)I think we should postpone the launch.
believe 🔊to have a strong view (creer / considerar)I believe this is the right decision.
suggest 🔊to propose an idea (sugerir)I'd like to suggest a different approach.
understand 🔊to see someone's point (entender)I understand your concern, but…

clarify 🔊to make something clearer (aclarar)Could you clarify what you mean by that?

Register tip
Professional tip: In English meetings, disagreeing must be done with a cushion first: "I see your point, but…" or "That's interesting, however…" Direct "No, you're wrong" sounds too aggressive.

📐Grammar — Connectors: because / so / but / although / however — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
These connectors make opinions sound professional. One rule: because/so/but go INSIDE sentences. Although/however go at the START of a contrasting idea.
📐 Connectors for Discussion
because
reason / cause
I agree because it saves time.
so
result / consequence
It's urgent, so I'll do it today.
but
contrast / exception
I agree, but the cost is high.
although
contrast (formal)
Although it's expensive, it's worth it.
however
contrast (formal)
It's a good idea. However, the timing is wrong.
because of
reason + noun
Because of the delay, we need to reschedule.
❌
I agree although the cost.  ·  However I agree because is important.  ·  The project but is good.

Register: formal vs informal
Informal: but, so, because
Formal: However, Although, Therefore
💡 Meeting English
'However' at the start of a sentence = professional disagreement. 'But' is more casual. Both are correct — choose based on the formality of the meeting.

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A2.1 · Opinions & Connectors
❌ I am agree with you.
→
✅ I agree with you.
Agree is a verb, not an adjective. Never use 'am agree' — just 'I agree'.
❌ Although the cost is high, but I agree.
→
✅ Although the cost is high, I agree.
Although and but say the same thing. Use one, not both.
❌ I disagree because the cost.
→
✅ I disagree because of the cost. / because it is expensive.
Because needs a clause (subject + verb). Because of needs a noun.
