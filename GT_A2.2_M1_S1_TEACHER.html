🔵Warm-up⏱ 5–7 min
THIS SESSION · Work News Update

Teacher says: "Tell me 3 work updates from this week or month using: already / yet / just." Student responds with real news from their company. Teacher models first: "Our team has just launched a new project. We've already updated our remote work policy. We haven't announced the Q3 results yet."

Why: Present Perfect with these adverbs is about RELEVANCE NOW. Real company news activates this immediately.
We've already moved to hybrid work.
Have you met the new manager yet?
I've just finished my Q2 report.
They haven't announced the results yet.
She's just joined the marketing team.

📐Grammar — Present Perfect: Already / Yet / Just — click to fill⏱ 8–10 min
📌 Teacher Note
Three rules: ALREADY = before expected (affirmative). YET = up to now (negative/question). JUST = moments ago (affirmative). Position matters: already/just go between have and PP, yet goes at the end.
📐 Present Perfect — Already / Yet / Just
ALREADY ✅
sooner than expected / done before now
We've already moved to hybrid.
She's already met the team.
YET ⏳
negative/question — up to now
Have you met her yet?
I haven't finished yet.
JUST ⚡
very recently — moments ago
She's just joined the team.
I've just finished the report.
❌
We already moved. · Did you finish yet? · She just joined. (need have/has)

Position rules
I've already finished. (middle)
I've just arrived. (middle)
Have you finished yet? (end)
I haven't finished yet. (end)
💡 Most common error
'We already moved' (no have) or 'Have you yet met?' (wrong position). Always: have + already/just + PP verb. Yet = at the end.

⚡Wrap-up & Error Correction⏱ 3–5 min
Common errors — A2.2 · Present Perfect Already/Yet/Just
❌ We already moved to hybrid.
→
✅ We've already moved to hybrid.
Present perfect needs HAVE/HAS. 'Already moved' alone is simple past.
❌ Have you yet met the manager?
→
✅ Have you met the manager yet?
YET always goes at the END of the question or negative sentence.
❌ She just joined the team.
→
✅ She's just joined the team.
Just with present perfect meaning needs HAS: she HAS just joined.
