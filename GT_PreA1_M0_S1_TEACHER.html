📋Session Guide
📌 Pre-A1 students need: maximum 8–10 words per session · zero pressure · visible wins · repetition without boredom · images over text · celebration over correction.
Session flow (45 min)
🔁
0–5 min — Warm-up: Show 5 images from last session (if not S1). Student says the word. No pressure — teacher says it first if needed.
🎨
5–15 min — Colors: Present one at a time. Hear → repeat → point to real object of that color in the room.
👋
15–23 min — Greetings: Role-play. Teacher greets, student responds. Switch roles. Keep it light and funny.
🎮
23–31 min — Match It: Student plays the match game independently or with teacher pointing. Reset and play twice.
🗣
31–40 min — Say It: Teacher points at images/objects. Student says the word. Zero sentences needed.
🏆
40–45 min — I Know This: Student marks all known words. Progress bar fills. Celebrate loudly.
Key principles for this student
🖼
Image first, always. Never say a word without showing the image or pointing to the object. The brain needs the visual anchor before the sound.
⏰
55 minutes max. End on a high — before fatigue sets in. A short successful session builds confidence. A long exhausting one destroys it.
🎉
Celebrate everything. "Red" said correctly deserves the same reaction as a perfect paragraph at B2. These students have failed before. Show them success feels different here.
🚫
No correction mid-production. If the student says a word wrong, say the correct version once naturally and move on. Never repeat the error back. Never say "wrong".
🎯
Session 1 Success Goal
Student leaves saying at least 4 colors and Hello + Goodbye confidently. That's it. That's a complete win.
🔍 Extra — I Spy!
Teacher says "I spy something [color]." Student finds the object and says the color in English. Then switch roles — student says it, teacher finds it.
👁
Teacher: "I spy something RED."
Student finds a red object → says "Red!" or points + says "Red pen! Red chair!"
🔄
Now reverse: Student says "I spy something ___."
Student chooses a color they see in the room. Teacher finds it. This is the first time the student produces English spontaneously.
📌 Teacher note: "I spy" is perfect for this level — it uses exactly one word of English (the color), it's fun, it requires zero grammar, and it feels like a real conversation. Do 5–6 rounds minimum.
👋 Extra — Greet + Color
Combine greetings with colors. Teacher points to an object and says a greeting — student responds AND names the color.
Teacher: "Good morning! What color is this?" (points to pen)
Student: "Good morning! Blue!"
Teacher: "Hi! Is this red?" (points to green object)
Student: "Hi! No! Green!"
Teacher: "Hello! How many red things can you see?"
Student: "Hello! One! / Two! / Three!"
