WordMeaning (español)Example

Key Verbs — M1
WordMeaning (español)Example
going to 🔊pre-planned future (ir a (planificado))We're going to reschedule the meeting.
will 🔊spontaneous decision/offer (futuro espontaneo)I'll take care of that — don't worry.
agree 🔊to have the same opinion (estar de acuerdo)I agree because it saves time.
disagree 🔊to have a different opinion (no estar de acuerdo)I disagree — the cost is too high.
clarify 🔊to make something clear (aclarar)Could you clarify what you mean?
wrap up 🔊to finish a meeting (cerrar / concluir)Let's wrap up — we're running over.
interrupt 🔊to break into someone's speech (interrumpir)Sorry to interrupt, but…
move on 🔊to go to the next topic (continuar)Let's move on to the next item.
Photo
M1 grammar summary
Going to: am/is/are + going to + verb
Will: I'll / she'll / we'll + verb (spontaneous)
Connectors: because, so, but, although, however
Adverbs: clearly, efficiently, directly
Phrases: Sorry to interrupt / Could you clarify?

✅ I'M going to present. (pre-planned = going to)
Going to always needs am/is/are. Planned = going to, not will.

→
✅ Although… (no but) / I agree (no am)
Two of the most persistent errors at A2.1. Both come from Spanish interference.
